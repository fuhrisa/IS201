King of the Blues

Who Was B.B. King?

  • Overview:
    • Born September 16, 1925
    • Died May 14, 2015
    • B.B. King was an American guitarist and singer that had a major influence in blues music.
  • Early Life and Influences:
    • Raised in the Mississippi Delta
    • Developed his distinctive vocal and guitar style influenced by various musicians including T-Bone Walker, Bukka White, Django Reinhardt, and Charlie Christian
    • Acquired the name "B.B." (for "Blues Boy") while living in Memphis, Tennessee
  • Career Highlights:
    • Hit record with "Three O'Clock Blues" in 1951
    • Popular songs include "The Thrill is Gone," "When Love Comes To Town," and "Sweet Sixteen"
    • Grammy Award winner for "The Thrill Is Gone" in 1969; recieved 16 grammys in total

Learning From B.B. King

Expressiveness
  • Tonal Variations: Study how B.B. King manipulates his guitar's tone knobs and pickup selection to achieve different timbres, from smooth and mellow to biting and aggressive.
  • Vibrato Techniques: Analyze King's use of vibrato, including the speed, width, and consistency, to add expression and emotion to his notes.
  • Dynamic Control: Observe how King varies the volume and intensity of his playing, from delicate whispers to powerful wails, to convey a range of emotions.
Mastery of Phrasing
  • Call-and-Response Patterns: Identify instances where King employs call-and-response motifs within his solos, mimicking the dialogue between a vocalist and instrumentalist.
  • Repetition and Variation: Examine how King develops themes and motifs throughout his solos, repeating and evolving musical ideas to create cohesion and interest.
  • Syncopation and Rhythmic Accents: Study King's use of syncopated rhythms and unexpected accents to create tension and rhythmic interest within his phrases.
Understanding Blues Form
  • Variations on the 12-Bar Blues: Explore King's use of variations on the traditional 12-bar blues structure, including extended chord progressions, turnaround variations, and modal interchange.
  • Melodic Development within Form: Study how King develops melodic ideas within the confines of the blues form, incorporating tension and resolution into his improvisations.
  • Transition Techniques: Analyze King's techniques for smoothly transitioning between chord changes and navigating key modulations within a blues context.
